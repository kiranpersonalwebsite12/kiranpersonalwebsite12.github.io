About

25 years at the intersection of AI and healing

Kiran Veernapu is an Engineering Delivery Manager and senior data architect at Intermountain Health, one of the nation's top nonprofit health systems. With over 25 years of experience, he leads AI-driven initiatives that improve clinical decision support, perioperative workflows, supply chain intelligence, and enterprise analytics.

His career began designing high-security automation systems for Indian military installations — translating defense-grade reliability into healthcare. Today, that same rigor shapes how he builds mission-critical AI platforms that clinicians and administrators depend on.

Kiran is a published author of 20+ peer-reviewed papers and book chapters spanning clinical AI, wearable health monitoring, healthcare fraud detection, and intelligent agronomy. He serves as a peer reviewer for Elsevier, Wiley, and Frontiers in Medicine, and sits on the Technical Program Committee for IEEE BigData 2026 and IEEE SoutheastCon 2026.

He is an invited keynote speaker on Generative AI in Clinical Documentation and Workflow Automation, and an active contributor to the EB-1A extraordinary ability space as a recognized international innovator.

The system integrates real-time demand signals, AI-driven replenishment forecasting, and supply usage pattern analysis across clinical units.
